Task 2.3.4.a.
Note that if \(\left[\begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{array}\right] \) and \(\left[\begin{array}{c} y_1 \\ \vdots \\ y_n \end{array}\right] \) are both solutions, we know that
\begin{equation*} x_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+x_n \vec{v}_n = \vec{0} \text{ and } y_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+y_n \vec{v}_n = \vec{0} \text{.} \end{equation*}
Adding these equations, we obtain:
\begin{align*} x_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+x_n \vec{v}_n &=\vec{0}\\ y_1 \vec{v}_1+\cdots+y_n \vec{v}_n &=\vec{0}\\ \Rightarrow (x_1+y_1)\vec{v}_1+\cdots+(x_n+y_n) \vec{v}_n &=\vec{0}. \end{align*}
Therefore, we may conclude that the vector \(\left[\begin{array}{c} x_1+ y_1 \\ \vdots \\ x_n+y_n \end{array}\right] \) is...
  1. another solution.
  2. not a solution.
  3. is equal to \(\vec{0}\text{.}\)
