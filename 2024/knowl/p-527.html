Let \(W\) be a subspace of a vector space. A basis for \(W\) is a linearly independent set of vectors \(S\) that spans \(W\) (but not necessarily the entire vector space). That is, for the set \(S\) to be a basis of \(W\text{,}\) we are saying two things:
  1. The set \(S\) is linearly independent.
  2. We have \(W=\vspan(S)\text{.}\)
