Observation 2.6.1.

Recall from section Section 2.3 that a subspace of a vector space is a non-empty subset that is closed under vector addition and scalar multiplication. If \(S\) is a set of vectors in \(\IR^m\text{,}\) we observed that \(\vspan(S)\) is a subspace of \(\IR^m\text{.}\)
Recall also that a linearly dependent set contains “redundant” vectors. For example, only two of the three vectors in Figure 14 are needed to span the planar subspace.
